## What This Adds to Requirements

### Beyond Traditional Datatypes

**Traditional needs (still required):**
- Creatures, characters, locations, times, weather, lighting
- Age, size, condition modifiers
- Spatial relationships
- Standard composition

**NEW requirements from creative categories:**

**Art Style Systems:**
- Rendering techniques: low-poly, pixel art, vector, voxel
- Display types: CRT, arcade, terminal, phosphor
- Print methods: woodblock, screen print, lithograph
- Historical movements: constructivist, bauhaus, art nouveau
- Digital effects: glitch, compression, scanlines, dithering

**Format & Presentation:**
- Instructional formats: assembly, blueprint, field guide, manual
- Data visualization: infographic, flowchart, chart types
- Sequential layouts: comic panels, storyboards, instruction steps
- Interface overlays: HUD, terminal, security cam, scope view

**Technical Constraints:**
- Color limitations: 4-color, 16-bit, monochrome, limited palettes
- Resolution limits: 8-bit, 16-bit, low-poly counts, pixel densities
- Medium textures: parchment, graph paper, newsprint, phosphor glow
- Era markers: decade aesthetics, cultural movements

**Perspective Tools:**
- Unusual angles: ant's eye, bird's eye, nadir, through objects
- Optical effects: fish-eye, kaleidoscope, stereoscopic, lens aberrations
- Frame devices: keyhole, window, scope, reflection, vignette

## Emergence Through Combination

### The Real Magic

**Traditional categories alone:**
- Dragon + sunset + storm = Nice but predictable
- Wizard + candlelight + mysterious = Expected

**Creative categories alone:**
- Low-poly + limited colors = Interesting style
- Propaganda poster aesthetic = Cool look

**COMBINATION is where magic happens:**
- **Dragon + Soviet propaganda style** = "Heroic dragon worker in constructivist poster"
- **Wizard + pixel art + CRT** = "16-bit RPG wizard portrait with scanlines"
- **Ancient temple + blueprint** = "Architectural cross-section of mystical ruins"
- **Forest + security camera** = "CCTV footage of enchanted grove, timestamp overlay"
- **Knight + assembly instructions** = "IKEA-style armor assembly diagram"
- **Sunset + glitch art** = "Datamoshed landscape with RGB corruption"

**This is the real test:** Do cross-category combinations work?
